Benchmarks and evaluation papers propose datasets, metrics, evaluation frameworks, and present results and analysis on LLM's capabilities on various tasks.

1. Benchmark datasets: several dimensions to consider
- What's the task input and output
- What's the size of the data
- What's different dimensions for the task covering challenges, such as domains, input types, input quality, etc. What's the distribution in each dimension.
- How is the dataset generated, manually curated or synthetic generation, and any audition phase

2. Metrics and evaluation: several dimensions to consider
- What's being evaluated
- What's the metric, and range of values
- How are the answers labeled? Rule-based, LLM-as-a-judge, or manual labeling

3. Analysis: several dimensions to consider
- Which models or solutions are compared
- What's the overall capability, and on different slices of data
- Any fundamental deficiencies of LLMs are discovered
